# FMEA Structure in Product Development

## Introduction

Failure Mode and Effects Analysis (FMEA) is a preventive quality tool used to identify possible failures, evaluate risks, and define actions before problems occur.

FMEA is applied at different stages of product development depending on the type of risk being analyzed.

The three main levels are:

1. Concept FMEA
2. Design FMEA (DFMEA)
3. Process FMEA (PFMEA)

---

# 1. Concept FMEA

## Purpose

Concept FMEA evaluates possible risks during the early product concept stage.

The goal is to select a concept that can satisfy customer requirements while minimizing potential failures.

## Example: Bicycle Pedal

Customer requirements:

* Weight <300 g
* Strength >5000 N
* Corrosion resistance

Possible concept risks:

Failure:
Design concept cannot achieve required strength

Possible causes:

* Incorrect material concept
* Poor structural design

Actions:

* Compare different materials
* Perform initial engineering analysis
* Review customer requirements

---

# 2. Design FMEA (DFMEA)

## Purpose

DFMEA evaluates risks related to product design.

The analysis can be performed at:

* System level
* Sub-system level
* Component level

## Example

Component:
Pedal arm

Failure Mode:
Cracking or fracture

Effect:
Safety risk during operation

Possible Causes:

* Material selection error
* Insufficient thickness
* Incorrect design calculation

Prevention:

* Design review
* Engineering calculation
* Prototype testing

---

# 3. Process FMEA (PFMEA)

## Purpose

PFMEA evaluates risks during manufacturing.

The objective is to identify process failures before mass production.

## Example

Process:
CNC machining

Failure Mode:
Incorrect hole dimension

Effect:

* Assembly problem
* Customer complaint

Possible Causes:

* Machine parameter error
* Tool wear
* Incorrect setup

Controls:

* First piece inspection
* Dimensional measurement
* Tool monitoring

---

# Relationship Between FMEA Levels

```
Customer Requirements

        ↓

Concept FMEA

        ↓

Design FMEA

        ↓

Process FMEA

        ↓

Control Plan

        ↓

Production
```

Each stage reduces risk before the product reaches the customer.
